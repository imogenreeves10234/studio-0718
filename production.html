Persona + Veo 3 production guide

Build ONE consistent, photorealistic, original SFW "worker-girl" persona and turn her into high-retention 9:16 Facebook videos. All craft — consistency, realism, engagement. 8 copy-paste Veo 3 prompts at the bottom.

The one rule that governs everything: identity is locked in the still image first, then motion is added in Veo. Build a reusable Identity Bank once and reuse it on every generation — faces that drift between posts kill the persona.

Lock her once

01Persona creation — step by step

The three identity layers (build order)

LayerWhat it doesTool
AnchorBakes her face + build into owned weights that hold across hundreds of imagesFlux character LoRA
ReinforceRe-asserts the exact face per-imageIP-Adapter FaceID + FaceDetailer
ControlLocks pose, body proportions, scene, wardrobeControlNet (OpenPose + Depth) + inpainting

Recommended path (the scalable, pro track)

  1. Design the hero face. ~1–2 hrs · ~$0 on sub
    Generate ~10 face variations in Midjourney v7/v8 (best "beautiful" instinct) or Flux.1 / FLUX.2 [dev] (best skin texture, and stays "in-family" for the LoRA). Pick the single best. Save it as your master reference + a fixed character-description you reuse verbatim forever (age, ethnicity, exact hair, eyes, face shape, build, freckles). Deliberately make her a distinct synthetic person — not a real likeness.
  2. Bootstrap the dataset. ~30 min · ~$3–15
    A LoRA needs 20–30 images; you have one. Use Higgsfield Soul ID or Nano Banana Pro multi-reference to generate varied shots from the hero, then hand-cull the ~25 most on-model.
  3. Train the identity anchor (Flux LoRA). ~30–60 min · ~$2–5
    20–30 sharp images (front / left / right / waist-up / full-body, varied expressions), auto-caption then refine (caption what varies, let the face bind to the trigger), one unique trigger word, ~1000 steps. Trainer: FluxGym on RunPod or fal.ai Portrait Trainer. Output hero.safetensors — your permanent anchor. This is the step where hired help pays off most; after it, you self-serve.
  4. Generate locked variations. ~pennies/image
    ComfyUI graph below keeps her the same person while scene/outfit/pose change. Swap wardrobe by masking only the clothing region and inpainting.
  5. Realism finishing (every image). ~1–2 min/image
    FaceDetailer → MeshGraphormer Hand Refiner (fixes ~90% of hand errors — matters for tool shots) → SUPIR upscale (rebuilds pore texture) → light film grain. Now it's monetization-grade.
Get the appeal level right — this is what broke the early attempts. She must be attractive AND suggestive: young, gorgeous face, plus revealing form-fitting styling — cropped top, cleavage, midriff, curves, denim shorts — with flirty confident energy. This is the thirst-trap look the viral accounts use; not modest, not "wholesome." The ONLY limit is the AI-platform line: suggestive, never explicit — no nudity, nothing showing private parts (past that, the model blurs or refuses). Don't lean on "imperfections / photojournalistic" language — it ages + plains her. Carry the same beauty + figure descriptors into every Veo subject line.

Beginner escape hatch (no ComfyUI): skip the LoRA + graph entirely. Run Higgsfield Soul ID or Nano Banana Pro as your whole pipeline — lock identity in ~3–5 min, generate every still via multi-reference, pay per image. Lower ceiling, no owned weights, but zero GPU. Best way to start; graduate to the LoRA track once volume justifies it.

Photorealism — the 4 tells to kill

TellFix
Plastic skin (#1 giveaway)natural skin texture, visible pores, subtle fine lines, subsurface scattering. Negative: smooth, waxy, airbrushed, plastic, beauty filter, 3d render, cgi
Flat lightingOne clear direction + real shadows: soft directional morning light from the left. Never ring-light flat.
Fake opticsName a real lens + DOF: shot on 85mm, shallow depth of field, faint film grain
Clean pixelsAdd sensor noise/grain finishing pass; upscale with SUPIR (low-res looks plastic because pores have nowhere to render).

What to generate

02Production still shot-list

Which stills to make so they animate cleanly in Veo. Input quality caps output quality.

Still-prep rules for clean image-to-video

  • Native 9:16 vertical, highest resolution, razor sharp, zero motion blur / compression artifacts.
  • Eyes on the upper-third line, modest headroom so a push-in doesn't clip the crown. Face in the central ~60% "thumb-zone."
  • Lead room = where the camera or subject travels. Leave space in the gaze/motion direction; tight-to-all-edges kills camera moves.
  • Eye-line into the lens for talking clips. Never use a profile as a talking source (forces the model to re-invent features).
  • No occlusion: no hand across face, no crossed limbs, no object cutting the body, no hair over features. Hands fully in frame, fingers countable.
  • Simple, stable, high-separation background. With start+end frames, keep ~60% of background shared to curb drift.
  • One camera move + one action per clip. Don't stack move + gesture + expression-change + lighting-change.

Shot types ranked by how cleanly they animate

RankShotUse for
★★★★★Close-up talking-head (chest-up, front or 3/4)Every hook, every lip-sync clip — your workhorse
★★★★Medium / waist-up (one gesture only)Talking + point / hold-a-tool / shrug
★★★★3/4 angle (body ~30–45°, face to camera)Default "hero" framing, livelier than dead-front
★★★Selfie-POV (arm out of frame)"Come on site with me" hooks
★★★Over-the-shoulder / reactionComedy reactions, "look at this" beats
★★Full-body / walking-toward-cameraShort (2–4s) establishing beats only
★★Wide establishingAnimate the scene (dust, machinery), not her

~80% of talking clips should start from a close-up or medium/3-4 still. Full-body, walk-ins, and wides are seasoning — keep them short and low-motion.

The checklist

Step 0 — Identity Bank (build ONCE, reuse as reference "ingredients", never animate)

  • Front close-up face (neutral, mouth closed, eyes to camera)
  • 3/4 face close-up
  • Profile (reference only)
  • Front medium / waist-up (tee + hi-vis + tool belt visible)
  • Full-body front (boots to hard-hat)

Wardrobe/continuity lock — write it down, reuse verbatim in every prompt: hard hat (fixed color) · hi-vis vest (fixed color/style) · plain tee (fixed color) · tool belt · gloves on/off (pick one) · hairstyle (ponytail vs tucked — pick one) · any fixed accessory.

A · Talking-head / hook stills

  • Close-up front, neutral mouth, eyes to lens (prime lip-sync base)
  • Close-up 3/4, slight smile
  • Close-up wide-eyed surprise (scroll-stopper)
  • Close-up skeptical eyebrow-raise (curiosity gap)
  • Close-up lean-in "secret"
  • Selfie-POV close-up

B · Action / holding-tools (medium only)

  • Holding a cordless drill up to inspect
  • Tape measure pulled out, looking at camera
  • Tapping hard-hat brim ("safety first")
  • Thumbs-up, big grin (payoff/CTA)
  • Pointing off-frame (leave lead room on the point side)

C · Movement (short beats)

  • Walking TOWARD camera, straight-on, clear floor (2–4s max)
  • Full-body hands-on-hips power pose
  • 3/4 mid-stride

D · Reaction / comedic (one expression + one pose)

  • Facepalm
  • "Wait, WHAT?" double-take
  • Shrug, palms up
  • Eye-roll
  • Big open laugh
  • OTS reaction to a consistent object
Per-concept budget: 1 hero still minimum · 2–3 stills for a single 8s beat (neutral → peak → optional B-roll) · 3–5 for a multi-shot short. Capture each expression as a neutral base AND an emotional peak so you can interpolate first-frame(neutral) → last-frame(peak) for a natural "builds to a reaction." Always attach the Identity Bank as references on every generation.

Speak film-crew

03Veo 3 prompt formula

Veo was trained on professional film footage — it rewards camera/lens/light/audio vocabulary. Vague prose = generic, plasticky, drifting. Specific = stable and intentional.

📐 Field order (front-load the camera)
[Cinematography] + [Subject] + [Action] + [Context] + [Style & Ambiance] + [Audio] + [Negatives]
FieldHow to write it
Shot + camera moveFront-load it: Medium tracking shot, 35mm, shallow depth of field. Pick ONE move.
SubjectPhysical specifics + materials: mid-20s, brown ponytail under a white hard hat, light freckles, orange hi-vis vest over a grey tee, worn tan tool belt. Material words give a light-reflection profile that stabilizes her.
ActionONE primary action, temporal order. The verb carries motion: striding vs ambling vs rushing.
ContextSpecific: sunlit half-framed suburban jobsite, sawdust in the air
LightingQuality + direction + source + temp: warm late-afternoon backlight through timber framing, soft rim light. Never "well-lit".
AudioDialogue after a colon, no quotes; SFX:; Ambient:
NegativesDescribe absence: no subtitles, no on-screen text, clean audio, no background music

Length: 3–6 sentences / ~100–150 words. Rule: maximal description of the static scene, minimal number of distinct events/moves.

Camera vocabulary Veo understands

Static (most stable, best for dialogue) · pan · tilt · dolly push-in (intimacy — prefer over zoom) · pull-back (reveal) · tracking · orbit/arc (Veo is notably good at these) · crane · handheld · POV. One primary move per clip — stacking moves warps.

Audio & lip-sync (text, not sliders)

  • Dialogue after a colon, no quote marks — also stops Veo burning in subtitles: She says: first coat goes on today.
  • SFX: a nail gun fires twice · Ambient: open-air site, wind
  • One line, under ~15 words, for a 3–6s clip. Keep the speaker visible + stable. Add no background music. Drop contractions ("I am" not "I'm") — renders cleaner.

Image-to-video + cross-clip consistency

  • Start frame — one hero still as first frame = strongest single-clip identity lock + native audio.
  • Ingredients-to-Video (Veo 3.1) — up to 3 references: a Subject image (locks identity), an Environment image (locks the jobsite), a Style image. Front + 3/4 + profile of the same character measurably reduces drift.
  • First-and-last-frame — give start + end image; Veo generates the transition (great for walk-in → stop → talk).
  • Hold ONE character across MANY clips: extend / last-frame chain in ~7s hops (up to ~20). On every hop re-state the full character description, outfit, setting, lighting, audio — the single biggest determinant of whether a series holds together. Hold the seed + same 3 references. Generate 2–3 options per hop, keep the least-drifted, then chain. Fixing drift at hop 2 is far cheaper than hop 6.

Known weaknesses → workarounds

  • Hands: keep out of tight close-ups; frame medium for tools; slow/simple motion; cherry-pick.
  • On-screen text: garbles — add all titles/CTAs/captions in CapCut post-gen.
  • Fast motion: smears — use slow, steady, gentle; speed-ramp in edit.
  • Multi-person: identities bleed — stay single-character, others background/out-of-focus.

Fast vs Quality (the money-saver)

Draft each beat in Fast (~$0.10–0.15/sec), run the prompt 8–10× to dial framing/motion/expression, lock prompt+seed+references on a keeper, then re-render only the keeper in Quality (~$0.40/sec) + upscale. On veo-flow-multi, parallelize the Fast draft stage across accounts; reserve Quality for locked keepers.

Win the first 3 seconds

05Hook & story cheatsheet

First-3-seconds rules

  • First frame = the single most striking frame of the whole video. No slow pan-in, no logo, no "hey guys".
  • First spoken word within ~0.5s; on-screen text from word one (60%+ watch muted).
  • If the best beat is at 0:15, ~70% already left. Open with the payoff/shock, then explain.
  • Target intro retention >70%; a viewed-vs-swiped ratio of 70–90% is where distribution takes off; under 60% collapses reach.

Structure for completion + shares

Hook (0–3s) → Escalation (→~80%) → Payoff (final 15–25%) → CTA/Loop (final 3–5s). Every beat opens a small new question as it closes the last.

  • Seamless loop — end frame flows into the first; viewers re-watch without deciding to.
  • Cliffhanger — end mid-tension with a partial result + a new open question.
  • Serialization — "Follow for Part 2"; the algo then serves Part 2 to Part-1 completers. Post next episode 48–72h later.

Five hook engines (pick one per video)

EngineExample opener
Visual incongruity / pattern-interrupthard-hat close-up → she lifts something heavy effortlessly
Curiosity gap"There's one thing nobody tells you about…"
Micro-story mid-drop"I broke a $40,000 machine on my first shift. Here's what I did."
Bold / contrarian claim"Everything you think about construction girls is wrong."
Direct question"Bet you've never seen a site lead do THIS?"

Spoken hook lands best at ~10–14 words in the first 3s.

Why the "attractive woman in an unexpected role" angle works (the SFW lever): the pull is competence + confidence + charisma in an unexpected context — novelty, not skin. A polished person confidently running a jobsite is a positive expectancy violation: attention snaps to her, she reads likeable not threatening, and it sparks a low-grade "is this real / how / why" debate that's a comment magnet. High-arousal emotion (awe, amusement) drives ~34% more sharing. Keeping it SFW both performs and protects reach (Meta throttles suggestive; novelty-of-role doesn't).

Named recurring formats for this persona

"Day ___ on the site" · "Things that break on a jobsite" · "Rookie vs. veteran" · "Guess what this tool does" — each self-contained but ending on a cliffhanger + "Part 2 tomorrow, follow." Principle: predictability + surprise (recognizable format, different execution). "That's literally us" relatability is shared ~5× more.

Captions & on-screen text specs

  • 3–7 words/line, 32–42 chars max, on screen 1.5–3s; break at phrase boundaries.
  • High-contrast, large, bold — must survive bright jobsite sunlight.
  • Keep text in the center third; avoid top ~20% (handle) and bottom ~25% (like/comment/share UI).
  • Reinforce the verbal hook with a text overlay (≥2s). Add all text in your editor — never rely on Veo.
  • A/B the hook, not the video: ship 3 versions with different first-3s, compare 3-second retention, keep the winner.

Numbers game

06Iteration & QA workflow

Gens-per-keeper

  • Stills: batch generously; keep only the most on-model.
  • Veo Fast drafts: run each beat 8–10×; several throwaways per keeper. Lock prompt+seed+references, then Quality re-render.
  • Extend hops: 2–3 options per hop, keep the least-drifted, then chain.

Reject a take if it has any of

  • Hands: extra/fused fingers, warping
  • Face warp / feature re-invention (head turns, walk-ins)
  • Foot-sliding / leg-swap (walking shots)
  • Burned-in garbled text/captions
  • Plastic/waxy skin, flat lighting
  • Wardrobe or hair drift from the lock
Anti-"unoriginal content" (Meta's #1 demonetization risk for synthetic personas): every clip must be genuinely new footage — new scene, new premise, new voiceover, new on-screen text. Never re-cut the same generation into many posts. The Identity Bank gives one recognizable persona; the A–E shot library gives enough distinct compositions to avoid the duplicate-content flag.

Keeping her consistent across a series

  • One source of truth: Identity Bank + fixed character-description + wardrobe lock, reused verbatim.
  • Every generation: attach the same references, hold the seed, re-state the full description on each extend hop.
  • Anti-drift finishing: FaceDetailer on every still; SUPIR upscale keeps identity while rebuilding texture.
