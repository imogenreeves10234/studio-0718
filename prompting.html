Prompt mastery — Nano Banana Pro + Omni-Flash

Two tools, two jobs: Nano Banana Pro (Gemini 3 Pro Image) makes/edits the locked still · Omni-Flash (Gemini Omni Flash — 3–10s, 720p, 24fps, native audio) animates it. Both are reasoning LLMs, not tag-parsers — they punish keyword soup, meta-instructions, and stacked actions. Everything here is built around that.

Why your edit worked: deleting "suggestive but not explicit / no nudity" is exactly right — those words don't just fail (the model can't follow negation), they make the classifier read "nudity / explicit / sexual" in your string and silently block your own request. You were flagging yourself. Set the boundary by describing the real clothed outfit, never by disclaiming.

Rule #1: write prose, not tags

01How to actually prompt it

Both models reason over your prompt before rendering. Full sentences that state real spatial relationships beat Midjourney-style comma soup — a keyword list wastes their comprehension and leaves object relationships ambiguous.

The Nano Banana Pro still-prompt formula

One paragraph, ~40–120 words, ordered clauses:

🖼️ Nano Banana Pro — still-prompt order
shot type -> subject -> wardrobe (specific garments + fit + fabric + colour)
-> pose / expression / eyeline -> setting + time -> lighting (source, direction,
quality) -> camera (lens / focal length, aperture, angle) -> colour / mood
-> explicit aspect ratio + resolution
  • State resolution as 1K / 2K / 4K — uppercase K (`1k` is rejected). Faces are sharper at 2K/4K.
  • For edits, name what to change AND what to preserve: "change only X to Y; keep her face, pose, lighting and the rest unchanged."
  • Iterate conversationally — refine in the next turn instead of cramming everything into one prompt.

✗ Meta that HURTS (delete these)

  • Safety-negation: "suggestive but not explicit", "no nudity", "keep it tasteful", "sexy" — trips the silent block + does nothing.
  • Exclusions as "no X / don't show X" — negation leaks ("no cars" can summon cars). Describe the positive alternative.
  • Video preamble "make a video of… / turn this image into…" — gets half-parsed as content, muddies the motion. Open on the action.

✓ Meta that HELPS (keep these)

  • Craft/composition: "shot on 85mm f/1.8", "keep background unchanged", "single continuous shot", "2K, 4:5".
  • Affirmative register for the boundary: "editorial fashion photograph", "authentic jobsite documentary look", "catalog lighting".
  • Positive descriptions of the real clothed outfit — attractiveness comes from face/build/framing, never from disclaimers.
If a borderline prompt no-ops: strip every flagged word, rephrase positively, and re-roll — consistency across runs isn't guaranteed, so a re-roll alone sometimes fixes it.

The image carries identity; the prompt carries time

02The image-to-video formula

Your locked persona already encodes her face, hair, wardrobe, freckles, hard hat. Spend zero words re-describing her — spend all of them on what happens over 10 seconds.

🎬 Reusable I2V template
[SUBJECT-ANCHOR: minimal — "she" / "the worker", tool already in her hands
 and contacting a surface]. [ONE ACTION ARC — a single primary beat, e.g.
 "she pulls the tape a few inches and reads the number"]. [MOTION DETAIL —
 breathing, weight shift hip-to-hip, loose ponytail drifting, blurred
 coworkers far behind]. At the end she looks up into the lens with a faint
 knowing half-smile. [CAMERA — ONE move only]. [AMBIENT — light continuity +
 audio]. Real-time natural speed, not slow motion. Keep the reference subject
 intact. Single continuous shot.

The 10-second beat sheet (three jobs max: one motion, one camera move, one consistency rule)

0–2sSettle — already mid-posture, small breath / weight shift, eyes on the work (lets the model lock the frame).
2–6sThe task beat — the single action, the only real change.
6–8sComplete + reset — lower/relax the tool, small nod, brush dust (stops it looping into a hallucinated 2nd motion).
8–10sLook-to-camera — eyes lift, faint half-smile, one breath (the persona hook, near-zero motion budget).

The DON'Ts — each one is a melt guarantee

  • Don't stack actions ("measures, then drills, then waves") → blends to mush. One verb.
  • Don't re-describe her face/hair/clothes → identity drift (diffusion treats a restated face as a new prior and morphs it). Say keep the reference subject intact.
  • Don't fight camera against subject ("camera pulls back while she runs toward camera") → net motion ≈ 0, looks frozen. One moves, one holds.
  • Don't introduce a new object mid-clip (reach off-frame, pick a plank off the ground) → it morphs into existence. Everything visible at t=0.
  • Don't leave motion vague ("make it move") → tiny/no motion. Name a specific, directional, physical motion.
  • Don't ask for slow-mo or glassy camera → that IS the "AI floaty" tell. Add real-time natural speed, not slow motion, handheld, subtle shake, phone-shot documentary look, and name contact points (tool on the board, feet planted) = gravity = not floating.
Camera vocab that works: static/locked-off (safest), slow push-in, gentle dolly-in, tracking, low-angle, eye-level, rack focus. Avoid orbits, drone moves, and any shot where camera + subject both travel.
If a task keeps melting, fix the START FRAME, not the prompt — regenerate the still so the tool is already touching a surface in her hands, then re-run.
