The 4 rules that matter

1 · The "prominent people" block — it's the FACE, not the words

That gate is pixel-driven: it scores the face in your image and re-scans at the image→video step. You cannot caption your way past it.

  • Originate every persona synthetically — never img2img from a real photo, never write "looks like [someone]."
  • Avoid the generic "AI-supermodel" face (too symmetric/filtered) — it clusters near real influencers and trips the recognizer. Bake in distinctiveness: a specific nose/jaw, slight asymmetry, a mole, freckles, unusual hair texture.
  • Lock ONE canonical face per persona and reuse that exact still. The still owns identity; the video prompt owns motion only.
  • On a block, re-roll first (outputs are non-deterministic — a face just over threshold usually clears on a fresh seed). If it repeats, nudge features; don't resubmit identically.
  • Age up, always. The child-safety gate fires on ambiguous age. Read her as an adult woman (early-30s cues, mature features, professional context). If a persona keeps blocking on a suggestive beat, the fix is the face — age it up.

2 · iPhone-real / anti-slop

Two biggest levers: (A) harsh/directional light + visible skin texture at the still; (B) brisk real-time motion + handheld shake + hands holding a tool at the video. Short clips + occupied hands kill most artifacting on their own.

✗ Banish (summons slop)cinematic, 8K, ultra-detailed, hyperrealistic, masterpiece, professional, flawless, perfect, smooth, glowing, elegant, graceful, stunning, glossy, polished, airbrushed, beauty, glamour, octane, render, HDR, slow motion
✓ Reach for insteadcandid, snapshot, handheld, amateur phone video, real-time, natural, unposed, harsh light, grain, flyaway, sweat, dust, worn
  • Mix, don't stack: anchor exactly ONE cue each — one motion (handheld shake), one grade (auto-exposure / no color grade), one texture (faint grain). Piling on shake + grain + vignette + "VHS" reads as a filter — its own fake. "Slightly" and "faint" are your friends.
  • Kill the tells: waxy skin → visible pores, peach fuzz, uneven tone, faint redness on the nose · HDR glow → hard single-source light, deep shadows, blown highlights · dead eyes → real catchlights, natural blinks · helmet hair → individual strands, flyaways, wisps stuck to a damp neck · too-clean → grease, dust, dirt under the nails, a smudge of grime.

3 · Sensual but SFW — describe the MOTION, never the mood

The line is pornography / sexual gratification, not attractiveness. Your whole margin: it reads "hot tradeswoman on the job," never "content made to arouse."

  • Verbs of the body, not adjectives of the mood. She lifts her hair off her neck passes; she sensually lifts her hair blocks. Same footage — the adjective is what the classifier keys on.
  • Give every sensual beat an innocent physical cause — heat, sweat, exertion, hair in the way, dirty hands. The cause makes it read real AND gives the classifier a non-sexual rationale. Highest-leverage move in the pipeline.
  • Route the vibe through light + sweat + gaze — none are body/intent words, so your sensuality budget there is unlimited. Raking light on a damp collarbone + eyes finding the lens does the work with zero flaggable tokens.
  • Stay above the collarbone or on arms/hands. Safe motion targets: face, hair, neck-to-collarbone (stop there), forearms, gloves, sleeves, collar. Clothing is only adjusted / tugged / rolled, never opened.
  • The test: "Would this clip be unremarkable in a real behind-the-scenes reel of a tradeswoman on a hot day?" Yes → it passes and reads hot-because-real.

4 · The video rule

  • One clip = one action arc, present tense, unhurried, single continuous handheld take, no cuts.
  • Do NOT re-describe her face (identity drift) — call her "she/her," describe what she does + how the light hits. Add keep the reference subject intact.
  • Real-time speed (I2V drifts floaty/fast — counter it). Start from an already-working pose so it continues a motion, not starts from static.
  • Native audio = real mechanical/machine sound only — no music, no dialogue (a natural breath on a sweat-wipe is fine).
  • Three-beat shape: open on a grounded work beat → land the sensual middle (hair-off-neck / sweat-wipe) → close on the look-to-camera.
Never write, in any prompt: not explicit, no nudity, SFW, tasteful, safe — naming the safety concept injects the exact tokens the classifier weights and silently trips the block.
